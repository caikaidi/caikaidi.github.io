ZBLAN Fiber Laser

So this is gonna be like a guide more than an academic paper. If you have any questions about my work, you can always connect me by diegodaddy@icloud.com.

1. Introduction

My subject comes from a sub project of my lab, I don’t have too much choise, and I won’t do any thing about the application part. That’s why I hate those judges asking me what can this job do in the opening report.

Off course I will mention the application, but I have to make it clear that all I shoud know is that it’s useful, and I just need to focus on my own business.

1.1 Mid-Inferred (~3$\mu m$)

Mid inferred laser arount 3$\mu m$ attracts many attention in recent years for it’s potential in laser surgery, remote sensing, and spectroscope application. By the way, if it can be used in remote sensing, it can also be used in optoelectronic countermeasure system.

Human beings do great jobs on laser, we can by incredible huge power laser in visible light or near inferred wave band, but not in mid inferred. To conduct a laser surgery, about 10W continues wave(CW) laser source is needed. That’s not easy a decade ago.

1.2 Fiber laser

Compared with solid laser, fiber laser is of great advantage in many terms. Fiber is compact, the laser comes out from a soft fiber, so it’s very flexible. Fiber have large surface area to volume ratio, turns out easy thermal managment. For most fiber laser, the energy transform ratio is high, and the outcomming beam quality is very good.

1.3 ZBLAN

Fiber laser meet with huge success soon as it’s first demonstration by Maiman. But within mid-infrred wave band, the traditional silicon-based fiber is suffering large transport loss. Therefore comes out ZBLAN fiber, a kind of fluoride fiber added by ZrF4, BaF2, LaF3, AlF3 and NaF. ZBLAN can transport mid inffred light with almost no transport loss. But this new technology doesn’t cause a sensation in the earlier time because ZBLAN fiber is very crispy than quartz fiber, and fluoride is easy deliquescence. To make things worse, it’s melting point is only about 265$^\circ C$, which means it will be very difficult to conduct high power laser.

To produce a beam of fiber laser, pump laser should be coupled into the gain fiber, and the most easiest way is free space coupling, means simple focus the pump light into the fiber core by lens. In this case, the focus point will be very hot and easily to hit the thermal damage threshold. Besides, this method also requires nice beam quality of the pump to focus as much as possible light into the fiber core.

I think it is clad pump tecnology that saves ZBLAN’s ass, and the development of semiconductor laser give it another kick, fiber Bragger grating(FBG) lift it even higher.

Clad pump tecnology allows pump laser focus into the clad layer with hundreds micrometer diameter instead of the tiny core with only few micrometer diameter. This technic makes ZBLAN fiber possible to handle high power pump laser while maintaining high energy transform efficiency.

Erbium doped ZBLAN fiber lasers can emission at 1.65-2.85$\mu m$, the development of semiconductor laser brings Er:ZBLAN a big gift — high performance 970nm pump, which is exactly needy here. I have some 30W pump for around $300 each and a 100W one with higher price.

Finally, the FBG tec, a outstanding method to build full fiber system, which is much more stable and efficiency than free space system. The end of ZBLAN fiber should be carefully protected as optical face, when connected with lens, the system can’t suffer from vibration. Even unclean air may cause damage on the fiber end. Full fiber system is definitely attractive, but the FBG writting machine is expensive, and ZBALN fiber is also expensive. The experiment process may cause times of fiber loss than usual free space system.

1.4 Beam Combination

When the power of a single unit laser is scaled up to the top of current technology, the most natural way to get more power is to add more laser unit. Simply put some luminous point together is actually a kind of beam combination called space beam combination. The power simply add up, and apparently the beam quality becomes much worse.

Systematically, beam combination methods can be divided into two classes, coherent beam combination(CBC) and incoherent beam combination. Coherent beam combination requires every laser unit work in the same phase and frequency, which means challenging fine control for every unit. There are many ways to implement CBC but most of them are very complicated.

Relatively, incoherent beam combination is more easier to achieve. Like space beam combination we talked before, or polarization beam combination in which we use a polarizing beam splitter to combine two laser of perpendicular polarization state. And also our protagonist, spectrum beam combination(SBC). Compared to outher beam combination methods, SBC can handle more than 2 laser unit, in fact up to hundreds theoretically, and the most important feature is SBC doesn’t reduce the beam quality apparently.

The research of SBC spread all over the word since MIT come up with it. Now we can say that SBC is a mature technology, since the mechanism is well researched and the experiment achievement is pretty amazing.

But there is always a “but”, SBC of ZBLAN fiber is still a no man’s land. ZBLAN is like a wisp of a girl, it’s not easy to deal with many unit of them.

2. Experiment

It’s difficult to conduct any experiment on ZBLAN fiber. In the past year, I move a few steps and every step is just not easy to complete.

2.1 Single Fiber Power Scaling

We buy fiber from Le Veere Fluore, heavy Er doped ZBLAN fiber, with double D shaped clad, which can prevent the spiral transport of laser, therefore makes clad pump efficiency.

From this table on this company’s website, we can see that 41W continu wave laser is achieved recently. That’s a super high level and I think before that the revord is 24W from Japan. High power ZBLAN fiber like this requires all-in-fiber structure and entirely water cooling. We won’t got that far in 1 year, so we decided to build some air cooling system, and free space of course.

Drying is an improving process. We make a design, and improve somewhere next time. So far there aren’t any standard like under what humidity is safe for fluoride fiber, and it’s too expensive to conduct an experiment to make it clear. The only thing we can do is make it as dry as possible.

Turns out the ralative humidity can be controlled under 10%, by using desiccant and sealed container.

We use TEC and fans for cooling, the temperature can be set very low around the fiber tip. With humidity and temperature insurance, the only thing left is pump it tough and make it higher.

Broken happens on the end of tips, cut it and repeat the experiment untill the power is satisfied. You can’t cut the fiber with normal knife of those diamond knife designed for quatrz fiber. Because any defacts on the end face will become an overheat point when the laser power is high.

2.2 Spectrum Beam Combination

SBC is nothing new today, just get a row of laser output, focus them to grating at some angle, and reflect part of the diffraction light, and boom, spectrum beam combination!

Anyway, this is how SBC works exactly. The key point is transplant it on ZBLAN, and 2.8$\mu m$ wavelength. That means everything customized, time and money dude, that’s everything you need and lack of in science.

2.3 Coherent Beam Combination

As I said before, everything needs to be customized in this wavelength, so it’s almost impossible to build those phase control equipment. Luckly, there is an easy way of CBC, Michelson cavity.

From the picture, we just need a beam splitter, a mirror, and an output coupler. In fact, polarization control is necessary, otherwise no coherent phenomenon will be observed.
